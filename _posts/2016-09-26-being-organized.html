I didn't have a clear direction so I ended up going to bed after adding a new weapon. The good news is adding new weapons is trivial, as it should be. The bad news is being unorganized is a motivation killer.

So now I've decided I need to keep a task list. Even if it's only for a days work, I should write down what I need to do to keep production flowing, or it will stop after a bit of aimlessness. I do like taking pen to paper every now and then, but I don't want to physically store this list somewhere, mainly because it can get lost, so for now I've settled on a simple text file that has the upcoming tasks. I've heard of apps like Trello that'll help track things but for now I want to keep it simple. This is really just to help me refocus after not working a couple of days.

My current task list. Simple and easy to digest


Soon enough I am going to have to spend more time designing the more minute details for my game. Right now I have a rough overview in my head as well as a few lists of features, but nothing on the player experience outside the actual gameplay. I'll need to design and program things like menus, settings, and transitions between everything. It's a beast separate from the gameplay that will take up just as much time.

I also almost didn't make a post for this, but I decided I want this blog to be as much part of development as coding is, so even if it's something tiny I'll probably make a post about it.
